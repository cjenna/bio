Jenna Crawford

Going from Zero to Hack Day Hero

past

Raised in the Midwest, I grew up with a techy dad, but I always preferred language arts over computer languages.

Sticking with my passion for prose and poetry, I graduated from Illinois Wesleyan University with a degree in literature. As you may suspect, writing beautiful essays is not the most sought after skill.

My job search landed me in Chicago where my communication skills built relationships with clients at Hewitt. When family drew me back south, I found a rewarding role as a psychology practice manager.

present

Although mental health care is a fulfilling field and practice management provided me with a breadth of experience, I found the constraints of private practice limiting. So, when I learned State Farm had a department dedicated to innovating, that was all it took.
I was hooked.

I've been part of an innovation team since August 2015, and I cannot fathom ever working outside the realm of innovation. Every day, I get to investigate, ideate and create.

future

I've always believed innovation can elevate the plane on which our business intersects with consumers and communities. To help actualize that vision, I know I will need to keep learning and growing.

The goals I've set are simple. My development plan focuses on acquiring more human centered design experience as well as coding skills. I also realize I need to branch out and extend my network.

Lucky for me, the Intro to Programming nanodegree at Udacity.com and the freeCodeCamp.org community tick all three boxes!
