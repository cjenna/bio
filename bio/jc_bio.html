Jenna Crawford

Going from Zero to Hack Day Hero

past

Raised in Bloomington, I grew up with a techy dad, but I always preferred language arts over computer languages.

Sticking with my passion for prose and poetry, I graduated from IWU with a degree in literature. As you may suspect, writing beautiful essays is not the most sought after skill.

My job search landed me in Chicago where my communication skills built relationships with clients at Hewitt. When family drew me back south, I found a rewarding role as a psychology practice manager.

present

Although mental health care is a rewarding field with opportunities for enhancement, I found the constraints of a small-scale operation too limiting. So, when I learned State Farm had a department dedicated to innovating, that was all it took.
I was hooked.

I've been an analyst in Innovation and RED Labs since August 2015, and I cannot fathom ever working outside the realm of innovation. I get to ideate, create and even help curate Tech Trends! This is my dream opportunity -- collaborating with the intent to bring far-reaching positive change.

future

I've always believed Labs can elevate the plane on which our business intersects with consumers and communities. To help actualize that vision, I know I will need to keep learning and growing.

The goals I've set are simple. My development plan focuses on acquiring more human centered design experience as well as coding skills. I also realize I need to branch out and extend my network beyond Labs.

Lucky for me, Zero to Hack Day Hero ticks all three boxes!
